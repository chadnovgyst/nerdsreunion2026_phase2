Phase 2 Ballot Guide

Record personal utility U(c) on a 0–100 scale for each finalist city and estimate your attendance probability P(c) as a percentage. Everything stays local until you export the JSON ballot.

Phase 0 · Externality

  • Each voter valued a hypothetical “+1 extra attendee.”
  • Responses combined via 10% trimmed mean (median fallback if <10 voters).
  • The shared externality constant is E = 24.4.

Phase 1 · Pruning

  • A binary vote trimmed 86 nominees down to the top 37 (plus ties).
  • That survivor list became the finalist pool you are filling out now.
  • Round totals (S1) were published for transparency.

Phase 2 · Your Ballot

Current step
  • Enter personal utility U(c) for every finalist.
  • Estimate attendance probability P(c); values are not auto-normalized.
  • Export JSON mirrors these entries exactly for Phase 3 submission.

Phase 3 · Aggregation

  • Coordinators aggregate attendance (Att) and expected utility (EU) across ballots.
  • TEW(c) = EU(c) + (24.4 × N) × Att(c); highest TEW wins after tie-breakers.
  • Tie-break order: higher Att, higher median U among P≥50%, higher overall median U.

Total Expected Welfare snapshot

TEW(c) = EU(c) + (24.4 × N) × Att(c)

Att(c) is the expected attendee count (Σ P_i(c)/100) and EU(c) is the expected personal utility (Σ (P_i(c)/100) × U_i(c)). During Phase 3 the coordinators compute these values from the exported ballots and publish the full scoring table.

Next-year accountability

If a voter reports a high probability for the winning city but skips the reunion without force-majeure proof, next year's ballot is scaled by 1 − Pwinning/200. Accurate percentages keep your future weight intact.
